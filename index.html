Motivation


Staying in a hostel comes with added overheads, including doing your own grocery shopping and laundry! One such tiresome trip to the department store, staring at all the millenials mindlessly fidgeting with their phones in the long queue at the cash counter made me wonder what if we could save time by decentralizing billing through the power of:
our smartphones, a magical wand that everybody wields.
Conceptually, every customer could simply scan the barcodes of their products and prepare a bill that they pay either via online payment or cash at the counter, and save themselves from going through the monotonous routine of a queue everytime.
Since this was the first time I had forayed into image processing in my freshman year, I focused on the image related procedure rather than the peripheral frills like bill generation and creating a product database.

Workflow


My implementation is centered around accessing pixel values, denoising the image, applying the decoding algorithm for a UPC barcode and returning the numeric code that is typically associated with a product in the database.
The entire image processing facet is handled through OpenCV as it provides a host of functionalities like in-built functions for denoising. I have also worked on minimal GUI windows that were coded using QT5 that manage the entire I/O and pop up once the program is launched from the terminal.

Digital Image Forensics


As a broad field it comprises of the analysis of the authenticity of an image file, processes to evaluate the presence of forgeries and determine the device which has produced the picture. With several obvious applications in legal and crime investigation it is an active field of research and study. During investigation one would look for anomalies in RGB color space or other color spaces, for discrete changes in a level of color and determine if pixels were manipulated. Typically, it would involve deconvolution, Fourier analysis, Wavelet transorms and other such procedures.



Source Camera Identification

Our field of research addresses a simple but potent problem statement: Given a query image and suspected cameras, can we determine which out of those cameras was the source of the image?
Well, it's simple to comprehend and potent because of the domain that it dabbles in and the ramifications it has.

Theoretical Concept

The basis of our study is the fact that every camera leaves a noise fingerprint on every image that it captures which happens to be characteristic to the camera. This fingerprint, known as the Sensor Pattern Noise or SPN, is a direct result of non-homogeneity in the sensor due to manufacturing defects known as Photo Response Non-Uniformity, the color filter arrays(CFAs) and post-processing algorithms for color interpolation, color correction, white balance adjustment, gamma correction and JPEG compression all of which contribute to the uniqueness of this fingerprint.

Current Work

Our work is naturally centered around the image processing and machine learning aspects of the experimental process so as to make an organic segue from our roots to digital forensics. My role included populating a database using the popular image forensics database Dresden Image Database that is built specifically for the purpose of development and benchmarking of camera-based digital forensic techniques. Our focus is on outdoing the present average multi-class accuracy by improving on the noise extraction and feature extraction & selection.




The natural scene; The extracted noise contaminated with scene details; Enhanced version with suppressed scene details

The main challenges that are faced are the presence of naturally occuring noise in an image due to either low-lighting or various photographical shortcomings that tends to mask the presence of SPN, and the improper extraction of noise which leads to contamination by several scene details. We incorporate several techniques that work on the wavelet transform of the images so as to suppress the presence of scene details and boost the Signal-to-Noise Ratio(SNR) thereby boosting the noise so that it can be studied properly. While we have achieved success on many levels, we continue to strive for a higher accuracy value and work on developing a novel approach that shows promising results.


Technical Setup

All development and experimental process is carried out on Linux/Unix(Mac) machines that run Python 3.5. Inital testing is done using Weka and a lot of the image processing and feature extraction is carried out using MATLAB.

Exploratory Research

Motivation

Picture yourself flying from your hometown to you favourite vacation spot. While you may kick back & relax and be thoroughly pampered by the cabin crew for the entire journey, there is this one person on the ground who sits upright in a chair with just one job, one that does not require much qualification or intelligence, stare at a monitor as the aircraft navigates the skies and make sure that it does not deviate from its flight plan. As astonishing as it may sound in a world of self-driving cars and Go-championning bots there doesn't exist any Decision Support System to act as a robust airline tracker that could make an operator's job of viewing a monitor for the entire duration a flight seem redundant.

 This is where I come in. The task at hand was to automate this monitoring task, of making sure that a commercial aircraft stayed on course in its designated air corridor and be able to judge if a flight goes off-course and flag such an anomaly for further invesitgation and probing via human interference.



Bird's Eye View

My role involved initial conceptualization and went on to incorporate populating a sample database to act as training and testing set, test the feasability via tools like Weka and finally build a working prototype of such a Decision Support System as a proof concept and to implement the mental picture on Python for approval.
I chose Python because of its highly flexible and comprehensive nature that allows an Agile Feature Driven Development approach.

Former empanelled design partners for Microsoft, FIDS is one of the leading design studios of India. Based in the bustling city of Mumbai, I was a summer intern here after my sophomore year. Some of the client projects that I had the privilege to work on



DISCLAIMER : The design process and the visual designs are bound under Non-Disclosure Agreement, but the amazing stories and memories aren’t. Scroll down for one such intern story :)



Week 1: Break it through, before you start making it.
As a first timer in the industry, I was asked to study and then create a set of style guidelines for the developers to implement the design of a finished project. As part of the drill, I broke down the elements, learned to closely observe the fonts, colors, grids and the use of softwares like Adobe Assets and Zeplin.


Client: Zoom TV Online Channel



Week 2 & 3: Let’s start collecting legos.
I was made part of the team involved in user interface design for a banking app. Since the project was very extensive in terms of deliverables, maintaining a consistent visual design language across the products was a challenge. I developed a strong command of typography, color, grid and font hierarchy as the project progressed.


Client: Bharati AXA Insurance

Week 4: Joining the legos.
Client wanted a smart, easy-to-fill form with more than 100 input fields for both it’s on-field agents and customers.

Client: Edelweiss Tokio Life Insurance Iterations of forms with well divided color coded sections, intuitive flow of questions, quick feedback and edit options, real time progress bar, and error correction were made. Various options of capturing data like audio recording using a checklist, capture using camera etc were also incorporated.


Week 5-8: Building a lego house :D
Now that the intern had settled well in, they made her part of an ambitious project to redesign the website of a global insurance firm.


Client: PNB GlobalMetlife Insurance
I joined the project when a set of personas with well defined attributes like research orientation, digital comfort, financial knowledge in accordance to the life stages were being sketched.


These personas were used to come up with various user journeys. The major pain points and moment of truths were then identified and user flows were decided.

We worked closely to build wireframes, visual mockups, and prototypes and even presentations to present to the client and communicate the essence of our ideas. Paper and pen sketches were used for low-fidelity mockups and Adobe Illustrator for high fidelity.

Visual designs were created following the Global Metlife guidelines. App mockups were created using the Marvel App.

All the projects I worked on had tight deadlines which included several iterations based on the continuous feedback by the client. Apart from all this, I also took part in a few hackathons and workshops, organized as a weekly routine of the studio.

Sharing food and free font resources over countless teas, lounging on bean bags and blogging while the Mumbai rains dribbled across the windows in all it’s glory, spending summers learning and growing couldn’t have been better.
